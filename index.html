About Giant Pandas

Discover fascinating facts about one of the world's most beloved and endangered species

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear species endemic to China, known for its distinctive black-and-white coloring. These gentle creatures primarily subsist on a bamboo diet, consuming up to 26-84 pounds daily.

Despite their classification as carnivores, pandas' diet is over 99% bamboo. They play a crucial role in the biodiversity of China's bamboo forests by spreading seeds and facilitating vegetation growth.

Once widespread across southern and eastern China, pandas are now restricted to fragmented mountain ranges in Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu provinces due to habitat loss and human encroachment.

Major Threats

Understanding the challenges facing giant pandas is the first step toward effective conservation

Habitat Loss

Deforestation and infrastructure development have fragmented panda habitats, isolating populations and reducing genetic diversity.

Bamboo Shortage

Bamboo flowering and die-off events, combined with climate change, create food shortages that threaten panda survival.

Human Encroachment

Expanding agriculture, tourism, and settlements continue to encroach on panda territories, increasing human-wildlife conflict.
